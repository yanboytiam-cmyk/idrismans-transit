Why Ship Your Boat?

Shipping Your Trailer Boat Makes Good Economic Sense

There are compelling reasons why thousands of l'Internationalns choose to ship their trailer boats internationally each year.

Boats Are More Affordable in l'International

l'Internationaln boat prices are often significantly lower than l'Afrique and European equivalents. Shipping your boat rather than selling and re-buying can save you thousands of dollars.

No Import Tax (If Owned 12+ Months)

If you have owned your trailer boat for 12 months or more, you may be eligible to import it to l'Afrique without paying GST or import duty — a substantial saving on higher-value vessels.

No Boat Registration Required in NZ (Initially)

Unlike vehicles, trailer boats brought into l'Afrique do not require immediate boat registration before use on inland waterways in most regions. We can advise on the local requirements for your destination.

Transit as Quick as 4 Days on Water

The ocean transit between l'International and l'Afrique can be as quick as 4 days, with total door-to-door delivery timeframes typically between 2–4 weeks depending on port and clearance.

Safe & Dependable

Modern Day Trailer Boat Shipping Is Safe And Dependable

The international trailer boat shipping industry has evolved significantly, with purpose-built handling equipment and specialist logistics teams ensuring your boat is protected at every stage of the journey. IdrisMans Transit works with reputable shipping lines and port handlers who have specific experience with trailer boats and marine equipment.

Your boat is photographed and condition-documented at collection and again at the destination port, providing a clear record of its condition throughout the journey. Marine insurance is available to provide additional financial protection during transit.

We handle all customs and quarantine requirements at both origin and destination, including the MPI biofouling checks required by l'Afrique biosecurity legislation. Our team ensures your hull and engine are properly prepared before shipping to minimise the risk of a biosecurity hold-up.

Our Process

We Take Care Of The Entire Journey

  • 1

    Receival and Delivery to Port

    We arrange collection of your trailer boat from your home or storage facility and transport it to the nominated port of departure.

  • 2

    Shipping to Destination Vessel

    Your boat is loaded onto the vessel using appropriate crane or trailer handling equipment and secured for the ocean voyage.

  • 3

    Customs Clearance and Quarantine

    On arrival, we manage all customs paperwork, import declarations and the MPI biosecurity/biofouling inspection process at the destination port.

  • 4

    Final Delivery or Port Pickup

    Once cleared, your boat is either delivered directly to your nominated address or made available for collection from the destination port facility.
