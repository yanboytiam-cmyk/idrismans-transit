Commercial Freight

Freight forwarding is the process of organizing the shipment and transportation of goods from one location to another, ensuring they reach their destination efficiently and cost-effectively. A freight forwarder acts as an intermediary between the shipper and various transportation services.

Such services provide expertise in logistics, customs clearance, and documentation. Benefits include saving time, reducing costs, and ensuring professional handling throughout the shipping process. Freight forwarders have established relationships with carriers and can negotiate better rates than individual shippers.

We offer air freight, ocean freight, road freight, and rail freight. Our team will work with you to determine the most suitable and cost-effective transportation option for your specific needs, taking into account factors such as cargo type, timeline, and budget.

It depends on the shipping method. Roll on, Roll off requires vehicles be completely empty — all personal items will need to be removed. Container shipping does allow you to place items inside your vehicle, as it provides additional security and privacy.

There are three key reasons: (1) Theft risk — items left in vehicles during RoRo shipping are vulnerable to theft; (2) Shipping lines won't accept responsibility or declare items left inside vehicles; (3) It avoids legal complications at customs — undeclared goods can cause serious delays and penalties.
